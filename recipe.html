Amala, Ewedu,and Gbegiri recipe

    AMALA:

  • Ingredients:
    1. Yam Flour(Amala)
    2. Water
  • Procedure:
  1. Boil water in a pot.
  2. Gradually add the yam flour to the boiling
    3. water while stirring continously.
  3. Keep stirring untill the mixture thickens and forms a
    4. smooth, consistent texture.
  4. Reduce the heat and allow it to simmer for a few minutes.
  5. Remove from heat and serve hot with your preferred soup.

    Ewedu Soup:

  • Ingredients:
    1. Ewedu leaves
    2. Locust beans(Iru)
    3. Potash(optional)
    4. Salt to taste
    5. Water
    • Procedure:
    1. Wash the ewedu leaves thoroughly.
    2. Place the leaves in a pot,add water and
      3. boil until they become soft.
    3. Transfer the boiled leaves into a blender
      4. or food processor and blend untill smooth.
    4. Return the blended leaves to the pot and add locust beans(iru).
    5. If desired,dissolve a small amount of potash in water and add
      6. it to the pot to help soften the leaves and enhance the texture.
    6. Cook for a few more minutes,stirring ocassionally.
    7. Add salt to taste.

      Gbegiri:

    • Ingredients:
      1. Brown beans
      2. Palm oil
      3. Pepper
      4. Seasonal cubes
      5. Salt to taste
      6. Water
    • Procedure:
    1. Rinse the beans thoroughly and cook untill tender,
      2. this can be done by boiling the beans in a pot or by using pressure pot.
    2. Drain the cooked beans and place them in a blender or food processor.
    3. Blend the beans untill smooth,adding a little water
      4. if needed to achieved to achieve a pourable consistency.
    4. In a seperate pot,heat palm oil and put the chopped onion untill transculent.
    5. Add the blended beans to the pot and stir well.
    6. Add water gradually to achieve the deired consistency (usaully a semi-thick soup).
    7. Season with pepper,cubes,and salt to taste.
    8. Cook on medium heat for about 10-15 minutes,stirring occasionally to prevent burning.
    9. Remove from heat and serve hot with Amala and Ewedu.
